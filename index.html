~Mega-Biodiverse~
With two-thirds of the planet's biodiversity and between 70% and 80% of all plant and animal species, the Philippines is one of the world's eighteen mega-biodiverse nations. With five percent of the world's plant species, the Philippines is home to a fifth of all plant species.
~Large amount of Fish Species~
There are around 121 indigenous species and 76 species that are considered to be threatened in the Philippines, which has a total of at least 3,214 species.
~Coral Triangle~
Earth's most diversified and physiologically complex marine habitat is the Coral Triangle. It includes parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, and Timor Leste. This vast ocean covers 5.7 million square kilometers, about the size of the lower 48 states. It comparable to Amazon rainforest diversity. Over 600 reef-building coral species (75 percent of all species known to science), 3,000 reef fish species (40 percent of coral reef species), 6 marine turtle species, and three-quarters of molluscs live there.
~Fishing practices~
The Philippine Sea has suffered greatly as a result of decades of damaging fishing methods like cyanide fishing, bottom trawling, and dynamite fishing. Since the 1960s, destructive fishing techniques have been widely employed in the Philippines, reaching their heights in the 1970s and 1980s when national fishing regulations naively promoted increased fish stock output without considering unintended consequences.
~Invasive Species~
Several foreign fish species have been introduced to the waters in the Philippines and have wreaked havoc on the native population of fish for many years ever since their introduction, competing for natural resources such as food
~Human Activity~
Various human activities are widely known to affect almost every aspect of nature as it makes our lives easier such as burning of fossil fuels, plastics, oil, sewage handling, but the consequences of these have snowballed to a degree that extreme changes are being felt by animal and plant species alike.
~Litter Management~
Reduce the amount of items thrown out onto the landscape and dispose of them in proper trash bins that are laid out and if possible, put them in proper recycling sections.
~Assist in cleanup~
Help local cleaners by picking up litter yourself even if it is as small as a candy wrapper, it will be appreciated by those around.
~Environment friendly products~
Purchase products that can be used and recycled such as brushes that are made of bamboo, paper bags or your own eco bag so that the impacts on the environment will be minimized as much as possible.
